Color-Coated Steel

Color-coated steel offers enhanced durability, rust resistance, and aesthetic appeal, making it the ideal choice for a wide range of industries including construction, automotive, and white goods. With various coatings to choose from, it provides a cost-effective solution for both functional and design needs.

Steel Coating Process

The color-coated steel process involves several key steps to ensure durability and protection. Below are the main stages:

Inception

The basis of most coated steel is cold-rolled steel. The sheet is coated with either a metallic or non-metallic coating to protect it from rust and corrosion caused by humidity and contamination.

Galvanization

Galvanization involves immersing the cold-rolled steel sheets in molten metal or using electrochemical processes. The steel is then cleaned, annealed, coated, and treated to ensure the adhesion of the coating.

Electrogalvanization

Electrogalvanization involves immersing the steel in an electrolytic zinc chloride solution, followed by the application of electric current to coat the steel with zinc.

Painting

Non-metallic coatings like paint are applied to enhance both the aesthetic and functional properties of the steel. These coatings form a protective film to resist corrosion and provide a refined finish.

Cut and Finish

Once coated, the steel is cut or formed into various shapes such as rolls, sheets, and strips. Additional surface finishes and treatments can also be applied for specific uses.

Coating Types

Ternium offers several different coating options for color-coated steel, each with unique benefits and applications:

Zintro

Zintro is a high-resistance coating created by hot-dipping steel in zinc, offering exceptional corrosion protection. Ideal for rural and harsh environments, it provides an affordable and durable option for structural applications.

Zintro Alum

Zintro Alum combines zinc and aluminum for superior corrosion resistance, reflecting heat and extending the material’s life. It is six times more resistant to corrosion than standard galvanization and is widely used in industrial units and facades.

Pintro

Pintro offers an extra layer of paint after galvanization, providing an aesthetically pleasing finish and additional protection against corrosion. It’s ideal for use in white goods and architectural applications.

Applications of Color-Coated Steel

Color-coated steel is widely used in various industries, offering both functional and aesthetic benefits. Some common applications include:

  • Construction: Roofing, walls, and structural elements.
  • Automotive: Vehicle bodies and parts.
  • White Goods: Refrigerators, washing machines, and more.
  • Architectural: Facades and decorative elements.
